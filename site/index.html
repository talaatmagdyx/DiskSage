A memorable safety model

Large is a size.
Not a verdict.

Every finding declares an exact boundary and one of three behaviors. Risk—not reclaimable size—controls what DiskSage may do.

01Safe

Regenerable and bounded

Narrow caches and downloaded artifacts can enter a reviewed Trash plan, then must pass immediate revalidation.

  • Eligible for explicit selection
  • Move to Trash
  • Fail closed on change
02Careful

Useful, but disruptive

IDE state, app support, archives, and runtimes may require reindexing, downloads, or a reset after removal.

  • Never automatically selected
  • Typed confirmation
  • Trash only
03Expert

Owned by another system

Virtual disks, models, and active runtimes stay review-only or hand off to the tool that owns them.

  • Inspection or guided action
  • No direct Docker.raw removal
  • Owner command preferred

Questions, answered

Know before you clean.

Does DiskSage upload my files or scan results?+

No. Scanning, rule evaluation, duplicate hashing, and cleanup happen locally. Diagnostic exports contain aggregate counts and redacted configuration.

Does it delete anything automatically?+

No. Safe findings still require review. Careful findings require explicit confirmation. Expert findings are inspection-only or guided actions.

Can I restore cleaned items?+

Eligible cleanup moves items to the operating-system Trash by default. Restore them with Finder or your desktop environment's Trash interface.

Why can reclaimed space differ from the selected size?+

Trash retention, sparse allocation, compression, copy-on-write clones, APFS snapshots, and filesystem accounting can delay or change reclaimed space.

Can DiskSage completely uninstall an app?+

On macOS, it can add positively attributed containers, preferences, caches, saved state, and logs. Documents, projects, and ambiguous shared containers remain excluded by default.

Can I audit the cleanup rules?+

Yes. DiskSage is MIT licensed. Its versioned rule catalogs, protected-path policy, IPC contracts, tests, and safety documentation are all in the public repository.
